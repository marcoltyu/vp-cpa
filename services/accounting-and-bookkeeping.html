Accounting and Bookkeeping Services

Venture Partners CPA Limited provides accounting and bookkeeping support to businesses requiring proper financial records, management accounts and reliable financial information.

Proper accounting records are essential for statutory audit, profits tax filing, management reporting, banking, financing and business planning. For many businesses, weak accounting records only become visible when an audit or tax filing deadline is approaching. That is usually when the headache becomes chargeable by the hour, which is not ideal for anyone.

How We Assist

We assist clients with bookkeeping, accounting records maintenance, bank reconciliations, preparation of management accounts, year end closing, accounting schedules, audit preparation, tax filing coordination and review of financial information.

We can also assist in improving the structure of accounting records, including classification of income and expenses, supporting schedules for receivables, payables, fixed assets, accruals, prepayments, related party balances and tax related accounts.

For clients without a full in house finance team, we can provide practical support to keep the accounting function organised and aligned with audit and tax compliance needs.

Common Situations

  • Start ups and growing businesses
  • Owner managed companies
  • Businesses without a dedicated finance team
  • Companies preparing for audit
  • Overseas owned Hong Kong entities requiring local accounting support
  • Businesses requiring management accounts
  • Companies needing year end closing and audit schedule preparation
  • Clients seeking cleaner accounting records for tax filing and management review

Our Approach

We focus on maintaining accounting records that are clear, organised and fit for purpose. Our aim is not only to record transactions, but also to help clients build financial information that supports compliance, planning and business decisions.

Good accounting records reduce audit queries, improve tax filing quality and allow management to understand the business more clearly. In other words, accounting should be a control tool, not just a storage box for invoices.
